"My this weeks goal"
→ Better:

"My goal for this week"

"give them my personal touch"
→ Good expression! Very natural.

"This is my first step kinda goal."

"This is my first major goal."

"This is the first step toward my long-term career goals."

I love taking photographs.

You can also say:

I am passionate about photography.

Or:

Photography is one of my biggest hobbies.

Some Advanced Vocabulary

You wrote:

"Backend is a very niche skill"

A stronger version:

"Backend development is a specialized field."

You wrote:

"AI has taken over frontend development"

"AI has significantly accelerated frontend development."

This sounds more professional in interviews because it's harder to challenge.

You wrote:

"I research all finance tools"

Better:

"I researched various financial tools."

focusing

backend development

Being able to say it smoothly will help in interviews.

## Question

Describe a failure and what it taught you.

### My Answer

This is one of my biggest failure and it is not a result or an output that i get but it is more of a behavioral habit. I usually tend to slack off while preparing for my exams most of the times before my first exam of the semester i tend to overestimate my preparation level as a result i dont do well in the first exam. But this is a common mental trap i fall into. Till now i havent been able to fix this habit. but this really thought me a bad habit that i still follow and still need to work on.

### Feedback

A Few English Corrections
❌ "i tend to slack off"

Actually, this is a very natural phrase!

✅ Correct:

I tend to slack off during exam preparation.

"Slack off" means to become less productive or less serious about work.

❌ "i tend to overestimate my preparation level"

✅ Better:

I tend to overestimate how prepared I am.

This is how native speakers usually phrase it.

❌ "this really thought me"

✅ Correct:

this really taught me

teach → taught → taught

Example:

That experience taught me the importance of consistency.

❌ "need to work on"

This phrase is already excellent.

Example:

Time management is something I still need to work on.

That's exactly how a professional would say it.

### Improved Version

One of my biggest failures is not a specific result or outcome, but rather a behavioral habit that I still struggle with.

During exam preparation, I often tend to become complacent and overestimate how well prepared I am. As a result, I sometimes put in less effort than necessary, especially before the first exam of the semester. This usually leads to poorer performance than I expect.

The challenging part is that this is a mental trap that I have fallen into multiple times. Even though I am aware of it, I have not completely overcome it yet.

However, this habit has taught me an important lesson: confidence should be supported by consistent preparation rather than assumptions. It has made me more aware of my weaknesses and shown me that self-discipline is just as important as intelligence or talent.

While I have made some progress, this is still an area of my life that I am actively working to improve.

I aim to level up to the highest role will gaining as much as experience i can through my corporate job. ### Feedback

Corrections
❌ "at the role of senior backend developer"

✅ Better:

"in the role of a Senior Backend Developer"

or

"working as a Senior Backend Developer"

❌ "will gaining as much as experience i can"

✅ Better:

"while gaining as much experience as I can"

Notice:

experience is usually uncountable.
I must be capitalized.
❌ "If by any chance i loose interest"

✅ Better:

"If I lose interest"

Lose = opposite of win.

Loose = not tight.

This is a very common mistake, even among native speakers online.

Advanced Interview Vocabulary

Instead of saying:

"I want to level up to the highest role."

You could say:

"I aspire to take on leadership responsibilities."

or

"I aim to progress into senior and leadership positions."

These phrases sound very professional in interviews.
