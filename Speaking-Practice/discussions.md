## Topic

Do you think AI will create more jobs or eliminate more jobs over the next 10 years? Why?

### My Response

I personally believe AI will accelerate work and output. People who might lose jobs would be the one who will not adapt to the AI revolution and be stuck to normal or old speed to get output. I honestly feel AI will help people a lot in getting the work done instead of replacing them as the bottom line on my screen says "ChatGPT can make mistakes. Check important info." so yeah it will require human skills and knowledge as well. I see AI more of a partner/buddy, just like how we both are.

### Polished Version

I personally believe that AI will accelerate productivity and increase overall output. The people who are most likely to lose their jobs are those who fail to adapt to the AI revolution and continue working at the same pace as before. In my opinion, AI will help people complete tasks more efficiently rather than completely replace them. Even AI tools acknowledge their limitations, which is why ChatGPT itself says that it can make mistakes and that important information should be verified. Because of this, human judgment, experience, and critical thinking will remain valuable. I see AI as a partner rather than a replacement. Just as we are having this conversation right now, I believe AI can work alongside humans and help them achieve better results.

### Improvements

A few improvements
❌ "would be the one who will not adapt"

✅ Better:

"will be those who do not adapt"

or

"will be people who fail to adapt"

❌ "be stuck to normal or old speed"

✅ Better:

"remain stuck at traditional working speeds"

❌ "I see AI more of a partner/buddy"

✅ Better:

"I see AI more as a partner."

Notice:

We say "see something as", not "see something of."

Vocabulary Upgrade

You used:

"AI will accelerate work and output."

A professional version could be:

"AI will significantly enhance productivity."

That's a phrase you'll hear frequently in business and tech discussions.

## Topic

What is one thing about yourself that most people misunderstand?

### My Response

I would be honest a lot of my friends dont usually get to know about my anger issues and crashdowns. IDK why but they usually tend to believe that i am calm and composed as i have been into difficult situation as CR and tour leader. Even when i had asked them to rate how bad my crashout was they tend to say "No you dont look like someone who crashes out"

### Polished Version

To be honest, many of my friends don't know much about my anger issues or emotional breakdowns. For some reason, they tend to see me as a calm and composed person. I think this is because I've handled several difficult situations while serving as a class representative and a tour leader. Even when I'm struggling internally, I usually try to remain professional and focused. Once, I asked some of my friends to rate how bad my emotional outbursts were, and most of them said, "You don't seem like the kind of person who loses control easily." Because of that, many people misunderstand me as someone who is always calm, even though I sometimes face challenges that they don't see.

### Improvements

❌ "I would be honest"

✅ Better:

"To be honest,"

This is a very common phrase in spoken English.

❌ "dont usually get to know about"

✅ Better:

"don't know much about"

or

"aren't aware of"

Example:

Most people aren't aware of that side of my personality.

❌ "crashdowns" / "crashes out"

These expressions exist in internet slang, but in professional English, you could say:

emotional breakdowns
emotional outbursts
losing my temper
getting frustrated
struggling emotionally
Advanced Vocabulary

You naturally described an interesting contrast:

External image

calm, composed, reliable

Internal reality

sometimes frustrated, stressed, or overwhelmed

Being able to describe contrasts like this is a hallmark of advanced communication.

For example:

People often perceive me as calm and composed, but I sometimes deal with frustration and stress behind the scenes.

That's a sentence that would sound strong in an interview or MBA application.

Speaking Tip

When you write, try to avoid abbreviations like:

IDK
btw
gonna
wanna

They're fine in casual chats with friends, but practicing full forms helps develop stronger English habits.

For example:

❌

IDK why.

✅

I don't know why.
