# Storytelling Practice

## Story 01

### Story Outline

What happened?
Why was it difficult?
What did you do?
What was the result?

### My Response

Okay, so this is about my Manali BE Trip. I was the tour leader for bus 8 and it was our 3rd spot Kasol. While travelling via our bus it was a pretty streamlined process, get into bus -> travel -> get off from bus but while we were in kasol we visited manikiran temple. And once we got out of the temple as per the instructions from the organiser I being the tour leader for bus 8 was supposed to contact my bus driver whose phone number i had not saved in my mobile phone. This created a very difficult situation for me as i had to manage all 25 people with me and also contact the bus driver. There was only one choice for me i.e. to gather everyone at one spot let them take photos and assign someone responsible and someone who can be trusted to look after the people while i run back up to the road and look for my  bus driver and ask him to wait at the gate of the temple. When i reached i was shocked to know that my driver was already ready to leave with other people from bus 6&7 to drop them off to the hotel. i explained the situation to him and lied that my phone was dead and i wasnt really able to contact him. He understood the scenario and emptied the bus while i ran back to the temple and instructed everyone to follow me to the bus. At last we reached the hotel safely will everyone on board, after getting down as a cover up i asked one of my friend to hand me his phone just so that i can save phone number of the driver for emergency and then saved it in my phone once i reached out of the bus drivers sight.

### Polished version

This incident happened during my Bachelor's trip to Manali. I was the tour leader for Bus 8, and we were visiting Kasol, our third destination on the trip.

Until then, everything had been running smoothly. The process was simple: get on the bus, travel to the destination, and get off at the designated location. However, the situation became challenging when we visited Manikaran Temple.

According to the organizers' instructions, after leaving the temple, I was supposed to contact our bus driver so that he could pick up our group. Unfortunately, I realized that I had never saved the driver's phone number on my phone.

This created a difficult situation because I was responsible for around 25 students and also needed to locate the driver. I quickly assessed the situation and gathered everyone in one place. I then assigned a trusted friend to keep the group together while I went back to the main road to find the driver.

When I finally located him, I was surprised to learn that he was preparing to leave with students from Buses 6 and 7 and head back to the hotel. I immediately explained the situation and told him that I had been unable to contact him. Fortunately, he understood the problem and agreed to wait.

I then rushed back to the temple, gathered everyone, and led them to the bus. In the end, all 25 students boarded safely, and we reached the hotel without any issues.

After that incident, I made sure to save the driver's contact number so that a similar situation would not happen again.

### Feedback

If I were interviewing you, the part I'd remember is not that you forgot the driver's number.

I'd remember that:

You realized there was a problem.
You stayed calm.
You delegated responsibility.
You found a solution quickly.
Everyone got back safely.

That's what leadership looks like in practice.

### Improvement

English Feedback
Excellent phrase:

"There was only one choice for me..."

This is natural storytelling English.

You can make it even stronger:

"At that moment, I had only one viable option."

"Viable" is a great professional word.

Instead of:

"I run back up to the road"

Say:

"I ran back to the main road."

Remember:

Present: run
Past: ran
Instead of:

"look for my bus driver"

You can say:

"track down my bus driver"

This sounds more advanced.

Example:

I had to track down the bus driver before he left the area.

Interview Insight

Notice how your original story focused heavily on:

"I forgot the phone number."

A leadership-focused version focuses on:

"How I solved the problem."

That's a subtle but important difference.
